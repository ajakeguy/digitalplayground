Where to start

    Bitcoin Whitepaper - written by Satoshi Nakamoto. Published on October 31, 2008

What is Bitcoin?

Thats the 21,000,000 bitcoin question. Everyone is trying to figure it out. The best way to start is by learning about the fundamentals. The learning never stops, but it's important to understand some of the fundamentals below.

Bitcoin Monetary Fundamentals

  • Divisble - 100,000,000 sats in 1 bitcoin that can be divided further if needed
  • Portable - Information that can be sent through a communications channel or stored in your head
  • Fungible - UTXO sets
  • Durable - incorruptible ledger secured by miners and verified by nodes
  • Scarce - Algorithmically enforced supply of 21,000,000 bitcoin that can't be changed

Bitcoin Technical Fundamentals

  • Decentralized
  • Fixed supply of 21,000,000
    • Predictable supply schedule
  • Permissionless
  • Peer to peer
  • Proof of work
  • Difficulty adjustment
  • Fungible
  • Censorless
  • Incorruptible
  • Verifiable
  • Public-private key cryptography
  • Trustless
  • Reliable - 24/7
  • Open source
  • Secure
  • Universal

Bitcoin Practical Fundamentals

  1. Freedom to receive
  2. Freedom to hold
  3. Freedom to send

Bitcoin Monetization Path

  1. Store of value
  2. Medium of exchange
  3. Unit of account (hyperbitcoinization)

The 10 Commandments of Bitcoin Self-Custody

  1. You shall backup your bitcoin seed
  2. You shall test your backups
  3. you shall Self-Validate with a Full-Node
  4. You shall not dox your UTXOs
  5. You shall not have single points-of-failure (decentralize your risk)
  6. You shall keep the Bitcoin Private Key Air-Gap
  7. You shall Coin-Control
  8. You shall KISS (keep it simple stupid)
  9. You shall not talk about (or ask others about) your HODL
  10. You shall stack more sats
